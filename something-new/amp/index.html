Something New

by Zachary Bluedorn

2017-07-30

"How long do these things usually last?" I asked the customer service rep in my broken Spanish. "Oh, they usually blow over in a couple hours or so. But the power? Who knows," he shrugged.

I tried to get service for my phone today. I'm a techie, and I feel completely isolated without service. Add in the fact that I don't speak Spanish well, and not having a working cell phone means I'm about one loud noise away from a panic attack.

Earlier in the day I made a plan for how I was going to ask for a service plan without really using too many words. The idea was to get to the phone store at the mall, point at my phone, and kind of make some gestures which would make it clear I needed data.

I left my apartment around noon, and two and a half hours later, I learned two things.

First, Panama City has traffic. Real traffic. Not "a little rubber necking after an accident" traffic, but more like "every intersection completely gridlocked, motorcycles coming from out of nowhere" traffic.

Second, the rain here means business. You see, Panama has two seasons - hot and dry, and hot and wet. And we're in the hot and wet season. When it rains, it's like a hurricane is tearing through, and in this instance it was enough to take the power out for the entire mall.

Just two weeks ago, I was sitting at my desk at Medallia, a software company in Palo Alto, the heart of Silicon Valley. I love Medallia, and am excited to watch it continue to grow. It has an incredible culture, full of people who deeply care about what they're doing.

But I've always had this burning desire to travel, to pick up Spanish, to just see what else was out there. And I just didn't think I could do it as a services consultant for a tech startup starting to build out its practice.

So I made the decision to take some time off and try something new, to see a different culture, and to study some of the subjects I never got around to in college.

I sold my stuff, cancelled my Hulu subscription, and moved to Panama.

So I'm new here, I don't speak much Spanish yet, I still don't have phone service, and I'm starting to see why they say that "things just take longer in Latin America."

Fortunately, I have lots of time.
